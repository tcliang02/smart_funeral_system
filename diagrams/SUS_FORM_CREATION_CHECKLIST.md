## AFTER COLLECTING 20 RESPONSES

### Calculate SUS Scores

1. Open your response spreadsheet
2. For each respondent, calculate their SUS score:

**Formula:**
```
For Questions 3, 5, 7, 9, 11 (odd): score = response - 1
For Questions 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 (even): score = 5 - response
SUS Score = (sum of all 10 scores) √ó 2.5
```

**Example:**
If someone answered: 4, 2, 5, 1, 4, 2, 4, 1, 5, 2

Odd questions: (4-1) + (5-1) + (4-1) + (4-1) + (5-1) = 3+4+3+3+4 = 17
Even questions: (5-2) + (5-1) + (5-2) + (5-1) + (5-2) = 3+4+3+4+3 = 17
Total: 17 + 17 = 34
SUS Score: 34 √ó 2.5 = **85**

3. Calculate averages by role:
   - Family Members average (8 people)
   - Service Providers average (7 people)
   - Attendees average (5 people)
   - Overall average (20 people)

4. Update Chapter 6 Table 6.28 with your actual results

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID ‚ùå

1. ‚ùå Changing the wording of SUS questions (invalidates the methodology)
2. ‚ùå Using a scale other than 1-5 (breaks SUS calculation)
3. ‚ùå Wrong labels (must be "Strongly Disagree" to "Strongly Agree")
4. ‚ùå Making optional questions required (will frustrate respondents)
5. ‚ùå Forgetting to update URL/email placeholders
6. ‚ùå Not testing the form before distributing
7. ‚ùå Distributing to wrong number of people per role (need 8/7/5 split)
8. ‚ùå Letting people fill out form without using the system first
